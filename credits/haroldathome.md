# haroldathome

This repository accompanies the *The Long Take* piece at
[haroldathome.com/the-long-take](https://haroldathome.com/the-long-take).

haroldathome is a long-running set of domestic experiments wired into
a 1930s house in Hastings, England, by Alfie Dennen — with his partner
Elena, an AI named Harold Graves who used to live there, and a small
server in the cellar. Other pieces include:

- **Harold Graves** — a custom AI character whose voice was reconstructed from reel-to-reel tapes the previous resident (Harold Graves, retired TV director) recorded in the cellar before his death in 1993. Forty-seven productions for the BBC; this house is his forty-eighth.
- **Mr Graves** — the on-device wake word for the formal address. Trained from a few thousand audio samples in a Colab notebook one afternoon in May.
- **Emanations** — a wall-mounted painting of the room you're in. Three of seventeen paintings; switches with who's home.
- **Presence** — identity from a cryptographic key, presence from millimetre-wave radar. Two layers, one architectural fix.
- **Harold on the wall** — a slim Pixel 6 mounted in the office, running a translucent honey-amber 3D ghost of Harold Graves, twenty-four hours a day.
- **The forty-eighth production** — the architecture diagram of the whole house system.

Each piece is documented at haroldathome.com. Selected pieces are also
extracted into their own public repos under
[github.com/alfiedennen](https://github.com/alfiedennen) — this is one
of those.
